01 · The problem

You run the mandi.
The parchis run you.

A busy mandi day is dozens of lots, split three ways — farmer, agent, mandi — and half your buyers on credit. Your whole system is a paper bahi-khata, a pile of kacchi parchis, and your head — and by evening the tally never quite closes.

🧮

The same split, forty times a day

Weight into gross, commission off, mandi fee off, net to the farmer — simple arithmetic, but repeated on every lot until a slip costs you real money.

📓

The bahi-khata is one spill from gone

Farmer dues and buyer credit live in a ledger only you can read. Carrying a buyer's balance across three mandi days is a note in the margin — until it isn't.

🤝

Who still owes whom?

You owe farmers their net; buyers owe you on the goods. Both move every day. Knowing your true position at any moment means flipping through weeks of parchis.

🌙

The patti won't tally at night

End of day you add up gross, commission earned and dues by hand. One transposed figure and you're re-checking the whole sheet under a tube-light.

02 · The insight

A mandi lot is the most mechanical money there is — weight times rate, minus two fixed cuts — yet it's still split by hand, lot after lot, on a paper parchi.

MandiBook's whole job is to take that one deterministic sum and run it as a cycle — book the lot, split it three ways, settle both sides — so the farmer's net, your commission and the buyer's outstanding are exact before the parchi is even torn.

03 · How it works

Book a lot once. The split and the patti follow.

  1. 1

    Record the lot

    Pick the farmer, the buyer, the crop, the weight in kg and the rate per quintal. MandiBook computes the gross the moment you type — no calculator.

  2. 2

    The split happens automatically

    Your commission % and the mandi fee % come off the gross → the net payable to the farmer. Your earning on the lot is shown right there on the bill.

  3. 3

    Settle both sides

    Pay the farmer against the net — part or full — and MandiBook tracks the running due. Record buyer collections against the gross and it carries the outstanding forward.

  4. 4

    Read the patti, watch the dashboard

    The day-sheet lists every lot with its totals; the dashboard rolls up commission earned today and this month, total farmer dues and total buyer outstanding.

How is the commission and mandi fee worked out on a lot?

You enter the weight in kilograms and the rate per quintal, and MandiBook computes the gross (weight ÷ 100 × rate). Your commission percentage and the mandi fee percentage come off the gross, and what's left is the net payable to the farmer. You see all four figures — gross, commission, fee, net — the instant you enter the lot.

My buyers pay on credit over several days. Does that work?

Yes. Record each collection from a buyer against their lots and MandiBook carries the running outstanding forward. The buyers board always shows exactly who still owes you and how much, so credit never gets lost between mandi days.

Can I pay a farmer part now and the rest later?

Yes. Pay any amount against a farmer's net and MandiBook tracks the remaining due. Each payout is recorded against the lot, so the farmers board shows the running dues you still owe across every farmer.

What is the daily patti?

Pick a date and the patti lists every lot booked that day — farmer, buyer, crop, gross, commission, mandi fee and net — with the day's totals tallied at the foot. It's the same day-sheet you write by hand today, computed for you and ready to read.
